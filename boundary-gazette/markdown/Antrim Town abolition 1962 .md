ULSTER SAVINGS CERTIFICATES
GENERAL REGULATIONS
NOTICE is hereby given that the Ministry of
Finance for Northern Ireland has made a Statutory
Rule and Order, No. 36 of 1962, entitled:—
"The Ulster Savings Certificates Regulations,
1962."
These Regulations, dated 13th March, 1962, con-
solidating and amending existing Regulations affect-
ing Ulster Savings Certificates, take effect from 1st
April, 1962. They include changes consequent on the
provisions of the Trustee Investments Act, 1961,
specifically authorising trustees to invest in such
Certificates.
Trustees can now invest in the certificates without
necessarily including the beneficiaries' names in the
registration, but alternatively they can still be pur-
chased and held in the joint names of trustees and
beneficiaries.
The maximum number of cerificates of the cur-
rent (Ninth) issue which may be held on behalf of
any one trust is 1,200 units, but a trust registration
is not reckoned against a purchaser's personal hold-
ing for which the maximum is also 1,200 units.
Copies of the Regulations may be obtained from
Her Majesty's Stationery Office, 80 Chichester Street,
Belfast, I. Price: One shilling.

PREMATURE REPAYMENT OF GOVERNMENT LOANS
PURSUANT to the Government Loans Act (Nor-
thern Ireland), 1957, Section 3(4), the Ministry of
Finance gives notice that it is prepared to accept
premature repayment of loans made from the Govern-
ment Loans Fund at rates of interest exceeding 6$
per cent, per annum, by the transfer to the Fund
of an amount of cash representing the present value
of the future repayments still remaining to be made,
such present value being calculated by discounting
the half-yearly or other payments at a rate of 3|
per cent, per half-year.
The Ministry may accept (subject to due notice
being received) repayment of principal at any time
without premium in the following cases, namely:
(1) Loans issued at rates not in excess of 6$ per
cent, per annum.
(2) Unexpended balances of loans where the Minis-
try is satisfied that the balance is unexpended
either because the scheme for which the loan
was issued was less costly than was anticipated,
or because it has been definitely reduced in
extent and also that application for repayment
of the amount is made within five years from
the date of its issue.
(3) Loans issued to individuals and subject to repay-
ment over a period of not more than 35 years.
(4) Loans issued under the Glebe Loans (Ireland)
Acts.

revoked.
