LOCAL GOVERNMENT.
ALTERATION OF BOUNDARIES OF
WARDS.
COUNTY BOROUGH OF
LONDONDERRY.
WHEREAS by the Londonderry Improve-
ment Act, 1896, the County Borough of
Londonderry is divided into five Wards
called respectively the East Ward, the
North Ward and the South Ward, the
Waterside Ward and the West Ward, the
boundaries of which are defined in the said
Act:
And whereas, by Order dated the 18th
day of June, 1925, the Ministry of Home
Affairs apportioned the borough councillors
and aldermen among the said Wards, and
such Order made certain provisions with
regard to the election of aldermen and
councillors for the said Wards:
And whereas it is enacted by Section 7
of the Local Government Act (Northern
Ireland), 1922, as amended by the Local
Government Act (Northern Ireland), 1934,
that the Ministry of Home Affairs may
from time to time make orders dividing a
borough, urban district or town into wards
and fixing the boundaries of such wards and
altering the total number of borough
councillors, aldermen, urban district coun-
cillors or town commissioners:
Now, therefore, the Ministry of Home
Affairs for Northern Ireland, by virtue of
the power vested in the Ministry by the
Local Government Acts (Northern Ire-
land), 1898 to 1934, and of all other powers
in that behalf enabling it, hereby orders and
directs as follows:—
1. Notwithstanding anything contained in
the Londonderry Improvement Act,
1896, or in any order made by the
Ministry of Home Affairs to the con-
trary, the County Borough of London-
derry shall be divided into three wards
to be designated the North Ward, the
South Ward and the Waterside Ward,
and every Ward shall comprise all the
houses, lands, and hereditaments within
the 
limits 
of the 
same 
and 
the
boundaries of such three Wards respec-
tively shall be those described in the
First Schedule to this Order.
2. There shall be elected on the 15th day
of May, 1938, for each of the said three
Wards into which the County Borough
of 
Londonderry 
is by 
this 
Order
divided the number of aldermen and
councillors which is set out in the
Second Schedule hereto opposite the
names of each of the said Wards.
3. The aldermen and councillors of the
County Borough of Londonderry in
office on the 15th day of May, 1938,
shall retire on the 16th da)' of Mav,
1938.
4. Of 
the 
five 
aldermen 
and 
fifteen
councillors to be elected on the 15th day
of May, 1938, the dates of their several
retirements 
from 
office shall be as
follows:—
(a) As regards the councillors in the
North Ward and the South Ward
two of the six councillors elected
on the fifteenth day of May, 1938,
shall go out 
of office 
on 
the
sixteenth day of May, 1939, two
more shall go out of office on the

been salted, cured, pickled, dried or

ordinarily prepared),

inguinal,

prepared in the form of sausage cas-
ings, and (iii) meat which has before
importation 
been made ready 
for
human consumption in the form of a
sausage or other prepared or manu-
factured article of food;
each of the Official Certificates of which
particulars are given in the Schedule here-
to, as evidence that the meat to which it
relates has been derived from an animal
which was free from disease at the time of
slaughter, and has been dressed or prepared
and packed with all necessary precautions
for the prevention of danger to public health.

label the recognition will apply only if the
label is securely affixed to the package con-
taining the meat, and if the label has not
in any other circumstances or on any other
occasion been used as an Official Certificate;
and where the Certificate is in the form of
a stamp the recognition will apply only if the
stamp is clearly impressed on the meat.

Official Certificates for meat imported from
the Union of South Africa are in substitu-
tion for the label of which particulars were
given in Part VIII of the First Schedule to
the Notice published in the London Gazette

IMPORT DUTIES ACT, 1932, AND
FINANCE ACT, 1933.
WHEREAS it is. provided by sub-section
(1) of Section 9 of the Import Duties Act,
1932 (22 Geo. 5, ch. 8), that, with a view to
obtaining information as to the condition and
progress of trades and industries engaged in
the manufacture in the United Kingdom of
goods of a class or description which, if they
were imported into the United Kingdom,
would be chargeable with a duty of customs
under Part 1 of that Act, the Board of Trade
may, before the commencement of the year
nineteen hundred and thirty-three and any
subsequent year, publish in 
the Gazette
Xotice that they will require returns in re-
lation to that year in accordance with the
provisions of the said section :—
And whereas it is provided by sub-section
(7) of Section 9 and by sub-section (2) of
Section 20 of the Finance Act, 1933, that
Section 9 of the Import Duties Act, 1932,
shall have effect as if references to a duty
of customs chargeable under Part 1 of that
Act included references to a duty of customs
chargeable under Section 9 of the Finance
Act, 1933, a duty of customs chargeable under
Section 3 of the Finance Act, 1925, and a
duty of customs chargeable under the Safe-
guarding of Industries Act, 1921.
Now, therefore, the Board of Trade in
exercise of the powers conferred on them by
Section 9 of the Import Duties Act, 1932,
as extended by Sections 9 and 20 of the
Finance Act, 1933, hereby give notice that
they will require returns in relation to the
year 1937, to be delivered to them in ac-
cordance with the provisions of the first above-
mentioned Section in respect of the manu-
facture in the United Kingdom of goods of
any class or description which, if they were
imported into the United Kingdom, would be
chargeable with a duty of customs under any
of the enactments following, namely :—
The Safeguarding of Industries, Act, 1921;
The Finance Act, 1925, Section 3;
The Import Duties Act, 1932—Part 1;
The Finance Act, 1933, Section 9.
