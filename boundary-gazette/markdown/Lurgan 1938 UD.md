WHEREAS His late Majesty King George
the Fifth, being satisfied that the Law of
the Province of Alberta as respects duties
leviable in respect of property situate in
Northern Ireland when passing on death
was to the like effect as the provisions of
sub-section 
(1) 
of Section 20 of 
the
Finance Act, 1894, was pleased by an Order
in Council bearing date the 25th day of
May, 
1933, to apply Section 20 of the
Finance Act, 1894, as in force in Northern
Ireland, to the Province of Alberta, as
respects duties leviable as aforesaid
AND WHEREAS by the said Section, as in
force in Northern Ireland, it is, amongst
other things, enacted that His Majesty in
Council may revoke any Order issued under
the said Section applying its provisions to
any British Possession as respects duties
leviable as aforesaid when it appears that
the Law of such British Possession has
been so altered that it would not authorise
the making of such an Order:
AND WHEREAS the Law of the Province
of Alberta has been so altered as aforesaid
and it is expedient to revoke the said Order
in Council:
NOW THEREFORE His Majesty, by vir-
tue and in exercise of the powers vested in
Him by section 20 of the Finance Act, 1894, as
it applies in Northern Ireland under the pro-
visions of Article 14 of the Government of
Ireland (Adaptation of the Taxing Acts)
Order, 1922, is pleased, by and with the
advice of His Privy Council, to order, and
it is hereby ordered, as follows:—
1. The provisions of the Order of the
25th day of May, 1933, which apply Section
20 of the Finance Act, 1894, as in force in
Northern Ireland, to the Province of
Alberta, as respects the aforesaid duties are
hereby revoked.
2. This Order shall be deemed to have
come into operation on the 19th day of
May, 
1937.
M. P. A. HANKEY.
