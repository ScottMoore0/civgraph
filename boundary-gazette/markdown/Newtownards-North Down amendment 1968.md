MINISTRY OF DEVELOPMENT
BELFAST-BANGOR TRUNK ROAD
(HOLYWOOD THROUGH-PASS)
The Ministry of Development hereby gives notice,
in accordance with the provisions of the First
Schedule to the Roads Act (Northern Ireland) 1948,
that it proposes to make an Order under Sections 1
and 3 of the Act designating as a trunk road ap-
proximately l£ miles of new road to be constructed
in the townland of Knocknagoney and the Urban
District of Holywood, Co. Down, from a point on
the existing trunk road south-west of Jackson's
Road, extending in a general northerly, north-
easterly and easterly direction to a point on the
existing trunk road near Croft Road.
The Order will also authorize the Ministry :
(a) to improve the .junctions which will exist be-
tween the new trunk road and certain roads in
the Urban District of Holywood;
(b) to construct a connecting road between the new
and existing trunk roads near Jackson's Road;
(c) after the completion of the connecting road to
stop up, to vehicular traffic only, the junction
which will exist between the new and existing
trunk roads adjacent to the connecting road;
(d) to stop up to vehicular traffic the junction which
will exist between the new road and Bath
Terrace;
(e) to stop up wholly the junction which will exist
between the new road and the pedestrian path
at the north-easterly end of Bath Terrace; and
(f) to stop up, to traffic proceeding in a north-
easterly direction, the junction which will exist
between the new road and Bangor Road near
Croft Road.
The Order will provide for de-trunking the super-
seded length of the existing trunk road following
the opening of the new trunk road to through
traffic.
The proposals are delineated on a map which,
together with a copy of the draft Order, may be
inspected by any person free of charge at all rea-
sonable hours during the period from 23rd February,
1968, to 3rd April, 1968, at the offices of the Minis-
try, Room 135, Stormont, Belfast, BT4 3SS; the
Down County Council, County Court House, Down-
patrick; the Holywood Urban District Council. Town
Hall, Holywood, and the Castlereagh Rural District
Council, Cregagh Road, Belfast, BT6 9EZ.
Any person may, within the said period, object to
the making of the Order by written notice to the
Ministry.
M. K. Harris, Assistant Secretary
